# C14.6: Uncertainty Quantification

> [Back to C14 Index](C14-Human-Oversight.md)
> **Last Researched:** 2026-03-29

## Purpose

Propagate confidence scores or entropy measures in responses. AI systems that produce outputs without uncertainty signals give users and downstream systems no way to distinguish high-confidence predictions from unreliable guesses. Google AI Overviews (2025) presenting hallucinated answers with no uncertainty signal is a canonical example of this failure.

Conformal prediction has become mainstream in the ML community (2025-2026): MAPIE v1 reached production maturity with a 2026 roadmap targeting LLM-as-Judge use cases, exchangeability tests, and black-box confidence score calibration with formal guarantees. Token-Entropy Conformal Prediction (TECP, 2025) integrates split conformal prediction with token-entropy statistics to provide finite-sample coverage guarantees for LLM outputs — a significant advance over raw log-probabilities. The Conformal Prediction with Query Oracle (CPQ) framework addresses the query-only setting for black-box generative models.

For agentic AI, uncertainty propagation through multi-step tool-calling chains is largely unsolved but advancing. The International AI Safety Report 2026 notes that AI agents "operate with greater autonomy, making it harder for humans to intervene before failures cause harm" — uncertainty propagation is the mechanism that enables meaningful intervention points in agent workflows.

| **14.6.1** | **Verify that** AI systems provide confidence scores or uncertainty measures with their outputs. | 1 | Overconfident predictions treated as certain by downstream systems or users; no signal to indicate when the model is operating outside its training distribution or producing unreliable outputs. Conformal prediction has become mainstream in the ML community (2025-2026) with libraries like MAPIE, crepes, and the Conformal Prediction with Query Oracle (CPQ) framework for black-box generative models. | **14.6.2** | **Verify that** uncertainty thresholds trigger additional human review or alternative decision pathways. | 2 | High-uncertainty predictions being acted on automatically without additional scrutiny; AI systems making consequential decisions in regions of the input space where they have low confidence. In agentic AI systems, an agent acting on a low-confidence intermediate result can cascade errors through subsequent tool calls. | Verify that uncertainty thresholds are defined and documented. Test that exceeding a threshold routes the decision to human review or a fallback path. Confirm thresholds are calibrated to the application's risk tolerance (e.g., medical applications need lower uncertainty tolerance than content recommendations). For agentic AI, verify that uncertainty in intermediate reasoning steps can trigger human review before the agent proceeds to the next action. | Connects directly to C14.2 (HITL checkpoints). The threshold should be set based on the cost asymmetry of errors in the specific domain. Threshold selection methods include cost-sensitive analysis, receiver operating characteristic (ROC) analysis, and domain expert input. Conformal prediction (2025-2026 research) can automatically define uncertainty sets with guaranteed coverage, providing a principled alternative to manually-tuned thresholds. Clinical AI applications are increasingly adopting conformal prediction for uncertainty-triggered review pathways (CHEST 2025). |
| **14.6.3** | **Verify that** uncertainty quantification methods are calibrated and validated against ground truth data. | 2 | Miscalibrated confidence scores that give false assurance (e.g., a model that says "95% confident" but is only correct 70% of the time); operators making decisions based on unreliable uncertainty estimates. | Review calibration plots (reliability diagrams) comparing predicted confidence to actual accuracy. Verify that calibration is measured on held-out data, not training data. Check that calibration is evaluated per subgroup and across distribution shifts. Confirm recalibration is performed after model updates. Verify that conformal prediction coverage guarantees (if used) hold across demographic subgroups — recent research (2025) shows that foundation model uncertainty can vary significantly across patient demographics. | Expected Calibration Error (ECE) is the standard metric. Calibration should be tested both in-distribution and under realistic distribution shifts. Conformal prediction provides distribution-free coverage guarantees but requires exchangeability assumptions. Temperature scaling is the simplest post-hoc calibration method and works well for many classification tasks. Conformal fairness analysis (2025, arxiv:2503.23819) applies conformal prediction to evaluate predictive uncertainty across demographic groups for foundation models, directly connecting uncertainty calibration to fairness requirements in C14.5.3. |
| **14.6.4** | **Verify that** uncertainty propagation is maintained through multi-step AI workflows. | 3 | Compounding errors in AI pipelines where the output of one model feeds into another; uncertainty being lost or ignored at pipeline boundaries, leading to false confidence in final outputs. The International AI Safety Report 2026 notes that AI agents "operate with greater autonomy, making it harder for humans to intervene before failures cause harm" — uncertainty propagation is the mechanism that enables meaningful intervention points. | Trace uncertainty through a multi-step workflow and verify that downstream components receive and use upstream uncertainty estimates. Test that high uncertainty in an early stage appropriately affects confidence in the final output. Verify that pipeline-level uncertainty is reported, not just per-component uncertainty. For agentic AI tool-calling chains, verify that uncertainty from each tool invocation is accumulated and surfaced before the agent takes the next action. | This is technically challenging and an active research area. Approaches include Monte Carlo simulation through the pipeline, Bayesian networks, or simple uncertainty propagation rules. For LLM agent workflows (e.g., tool-calling chains), uncertainty propagation is largely unsolved but advancing: the Conformal Prediction with Query Oracle (CPQ, 2025) framework addresses the query-only setting for black-box generative models, characterizing the optimal interplay between coverage, test-time query budget, and informativeness. Conformal prediction methods that work without model retraining are particularly relevant for pipeline settings where individual components are black boxes. Rated L3 due to lack of mature tooling, though the pace of conformal prediction research (2025-2026) may close this gap. |
