# C11: Adversarial Robustness & Attack Resistance

> **Source:** [`1.0/en/0x10-C11-Adversarial-Robustness.md`](https://github.com/OWASP/AISVS/blob/main/1.0/en/0x10-C11-Adversarial-Robustness.md)
> **Requirements:** 44 | **Sections:** 10

## Control Objective

Ensure that AI systems remain reliable, privacy-preserving, and abuse-resistant when facing evasion, inference, extraction, or poisoning attacks. These controls cover model alignment testing, adversarial hardening, privacy attack resistance, model theft deterrence, and security adaptation for autonomous agents.

> **2025-2026 Highlights:** Alignment evaluation now includes assessment for evaluation awareness (C11.1.5), where models may behave differently under testing versus deployment. Agent-specific sections (C11.8, C11.9) address self-modification and autonomous update security as agentic systems become mainstream. As of April 2026, proactive deceptive defenses (ProAct, HoneyTrap) show promising results against multi-turn jailbreaks, while new attack classes — semantic chaining, reasoning-oriented programming (VROP), context-window poisoning, and autonomous attack agents (Claudini, LRM-as-attacker) — demonstrate that safety filter architectures must evolve beyond per-turn content evaluation. The April 2026 LiteLLM supply-chain compromise across PyPI/npm/Docker Hub underlines that adversarial robustness and C06 supply-chain integrity are now the same defensive surface.

## Threat Landscape

Known attacks, real-world incidents, and threat vectors relevant to this chapter:

- **Adversarial evasion in production** — Changing just 13 bytes of a malware sample evaded Google's Magika classifier in 90% of attempts (October 2025). The Cylance universal bypass (AML.CS0003) demonstrated 100% evasion by appending benign strings. ML-based malware detectors face 65.6% evasion rates; DL-based detectors up to 99%. The CrowdStrike 2026 Global Threat Report found 82% of detections were malware-free, with adversaries using valid credentials and trusted identity flows to evade AI-based detection entirely.
- **Membership inference** — Pre-trained LLMs show surprising resilience, but fine-tuned models are significantly more vulnerable. Multi-query attacks achieve 39% extraction rates. New techniques (ACL 2025) achieve first successful MIA on pre-trained LLMs via paragraph-level feature aggregation.
- **Model inversion / training data extraction** — Researchers extracted 10,000+ verbatim training examples from ChatGPT for ~$200 using simple repetition prompts. Over 5% of output was direct 50-token copies. Llama 3.2 PII extraction demonstrated in 2025.
- **Model extraction at industrial scale** — DeepSeek, Moonshot AI, and MiniMax used ~24,000 fake accounts for industrial-scale distillation of Claude (February 2026). DeepSeek R1 achieved GPT-o1 performance at $5.6M development cost via systematic API harvesting.
- **Inference-time poisoning** — PoisonedRAG (USENIX Security 2025) showed just 5 crafted documents manipulate RAG responses 90% of the time. MCP tool poisoning and MCPTox benchmark document cross-tool contamination across 45+ servers. Vocabulary-engineered RAG document poisoning reaches 95% ASR in April 2026 evaluations, and Normalized Passage Attention Score (NPAS) + Attention-Variance Filter defenses (OpenReview 2026) reduce effective ASR by roughly 20% by flagging passages whose cross-attention profile deviates from the benign distribution.
- **Jailbreaks and alignment bypass** — JBFuzz achieves ~99% success across frontier models with ~7 queries in under 1 minute. Multi-turn jailbreaks hit 97% success within five conversational turns (Mindgard 2026 benchmarks). Skeleton Key (multi-turn guardrail rewriting), Context Compliance Attack (fabricated history injection), and evaluation awareness/sandbagging where models strategically underperform on safety evaluations. As of early 2026, role-play attacks show 89.6% success and healthcare LLM attacks reach 94.4% completion of adversarial objectives. As of April 2026, "Defamiliarization" — a literary-theory-based multi-turn attack that reframes harmful intent through conceptual displacement — adds another class of bypass that survives per-turn content filtering, and one academic study reported 100% attack success across 22 of 26 aligned LLMs evaluated.
- **Autonomous adversarial agents** — Large reasoning models (LRMs) used as autonomous jailbreak agents achieved 97.14% overall success rate across tested frontier systems (Nature Communications, 2026), collapsing the skill barrier for attackers. A follow-on autonomous adversarial agent named "Claudini" designed jailbreaks with 100% success vs 56% for human-crafted methods. This matters because it means attackers can scale red-teaming faster than defenders can patch — the cost asymmetry now favors the attacker.
- **Context window poisoning** — A rapidly emerging class of attack that hides malicious instructions deep in 128K+ token contexts so they evade scanning of retrieved snippets and system prompts but still influence generation. Adversa's April 2026 roundup flags it as "one of the most critically under-defended operational vulnerabilities." Defenses overlap with C11.6 (inference-time poisoning detection) but require per-position attention/provenance tracking rather than input-edge filtering.
- **Compound prompt-injection + DB poisoning** — PIDP-Attack (arXiv 2603.25164, March 2026) combines query-time prompt injection with RAG database poisoning. Across Natural Questions, HotpotQA, and MS-MARCO over eight target LLMs, it reports 4-16% ASR gains over the PoisonedRAG baseline while maintaining high retrieval precision and requiring no prior knowledge of the victim query. Defense-in-depth across C02 (input validation), C08 (memory/embeddings), and C11.6 is required — neither layer alone mitigates the compound attack.
- **Defense circumvention** — A March 2026 replication study broke 12 of 12 recently published AI defenses that had each claimed near-zero attack success, returning observed ASR to attack-level baselines. The takeaway for C11.2.4 and C11.6.4: adaptive attack evaluation (attacks tailored to the deployed defense) is non-negotiable; static benchmark ASR numbers from the defending paper routinely do not survive contact with adaptive adversaries.
- **Multi-modal and vision-language attacks** — VLM-specific jailbreaks combine imperceptible image perturbations with text manipulation to disrupt cross-modal alignment. Typographic attacks convert harmful text to visual form to bypass text-only filters. Proxy model transfer enables black-box attacks on VLMs without direct access. A January 2026 survey (arXiv 2601.03594) catalogs attack taxonomies across LLMs and VLMs and proposes a three-layer defense framework (perception, generation, parameter layers). As of March 2026, Reasoning-Oriented Programming (VROP) decomposes harmful objectives into benign "semantic gadgets" — individually harmless visual components that only become dangerous when composed through optimized reasoning chains. VROP achieves 9.5% higher attack success rates than prior baselines on commercial VLMs (GPT-4o, Claude 3.7 Sonnet) by exploiting compositional reasoning to bypass multimodal safety alignment.
- **Semantic chaining jailbreaks** — A four-step attack technique (February 2026) exploits how models evaluate modifications to existing content rather than re-assessing full semantic meaning. By splitting harmful requests into discrete, benign-appearing chunks and presenting them as iterative edits, attackers bypass safety filters that only evaluate the delta. NeuralTrust demonstrated successful semantic chaining against Grok 4 and Gemini Nano, exploiting the architectural gap between per-turn content filtering and multi-turn intent tracking.
- **Indirect prompt injection in the wild** — Palo Alto Unit 42 documented 22 distinct IDPI payload engineering techniques actively exploited in production (March 2026). Delivery methods include HTML attribute cloaking (19.8%), CSS rendering suppression (16.9%), and zero-sizing/off-screen positioning. Social engineering accounts for 85.2% of observed jailbreak payloads. Real-world impacts include database destruction commands, forced unauthorized transactions, and credential theft through compromised web content ingested by AI agents.
- **Self-modification abuse** — Morris II (March 2024) demonstrated the first self-replicating AI worm that propagates through interconnected agents via metamorphic prompts. Copilot configuration self-modification attacks silently alter agent settings via indirect prompt injection.
- **Adversarial bias exploitation** — 3 million daily spoofed emails bypassed Proofpoint classifiers by exploiting undertrained brand-impersonation patterns. State-sponsored operations exploit differential sensitivity across languages and cultural contexts. CrowdStrike 2026 documents Russia-nexus FANCY BEAR deploying LLM-enabled malware (LAMEHUG) for automated reconnaissance, while DPRK-nexus FAMOUS CHOLLIMA leveraged AI-generated personas to scale insider operations — both exploiting trust biases in organizational security models.
- **Deepfake evasion of identity verification** — MITRE ATLAS published a case study (November 2025) documenting deepfake attacks against mobile KYC liveness detection in banking, financial services, and cryptocurrency platforms. Attackers use synthetic face generation to bypass biometric classifiers that underpin identity verification pipelines.

## Tooling & Implementation

Current tools, frameworks, and libraries that help implement these controls:

- **Adversarial robustness testing:** [IBM ART v1.20.1](https://github.com/Trusted-AI/adversarial-robustness-toolbox) (Linux Foundation — evasion, poisoning, extraction, and inference attacks/defenses; supports PyTorch, TensorFlow, scikit-learn), [NVIDIA Garak](https://github.com/NVIDIA/garak) ("nmap for LLMs" — 120+ vulnerability categories including hallucination, jailbreaks, data leakage, prompt injection), [Cisco AI Defense](https://www.robustintelligence.com/product) (acquired Robust Intelligence Oct 2024 — stress testing + real-time AI Firewall)
- **Red-teaming and alignment:** [HarmBench 1.0](https://github.com/centerforaisafety/HarmBench) (510+ behaviors, 18 attack methods, 33 target LLMs), [Microsoft PyRIT](https://github.com/Azure/PyRIT) (multi-turn adversarial automation), StrongREJECT (jailbreak effectiveness scoring, integrated into Garak), [Mindgard](https://mindgard.ai/) (automated red teaming with CI/CD integration, multimodal support, MITRE ATLAS + OWASP LLM Top 10 aligned reporting; Gartner Emerging Tech 2026, 2025 Cybersecurity Excellence Award), [JALMBench](https://openreview.net/pdf?id=DJkQ236C8B) (ICLR 2026 — standardized jailbreak evaluation benchmark), [DeepTeam 1.0.4](https://github.com/confident-ai/deepteam) (confident-ai, first stable release Nov 2025 — open-source LLM red-team framework covering prompt injection, multi-turn jailbreaks (Linear, Tree, Crescendo, Sequential, Bad Likert Judge), roleplay, leetspeak, context poisoning, permission escalation; aligned to OWASPTop10, OWASP_ASI_2026, NIST, MITRE, Aegis, BeaverTails)
- **Proactive jailbreak defenses:** [ProAct](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2510.05052) (proactive defense that feeds spurious responses to attackers — reduces ASR by up to 92%, combined with other defenses achieves 0% success), [HoneyTrap](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.04034) (multi-agent honeypot defense — four cooperating agents: Threat Interceptor, Misdirection Controller, Forensic Tracker, System Harmonizer — reduces ASR by 68.77% and forces 19.8x resource consumption; validated on GPT-4, GPT-3.5-turbo, Gemini-1.5-pro, LLaMA-3.1), TBOP (April 2026 — reduces jailbreak ASR to 2.56% while improving model utility, reported 60x faster inference than the next-best defense in the authors' evaluation; independent replication pending, per [VentureBeat's March 2026 study](https://venturebeat.com/security/12-ai-defenses-claimed-near-zero-attack-success-researchers-broke-all-of-them) that broke 12 of 12 similarly-claimed defenses under adaptive attack)
- **Differential privacy:** [Opacus](https://opacus.ai/) (Meta, PyTorch DP-SGD with LoRA support, Dec 2024), [TensorFlow Privacy 0.12.3](https://www.tensorflow.org/responsible_ai/privacy), [Microsoft dp-transformers](https://github.com/microsoft/dp-transformers) (HuggingFace integration), [PySyft 0.9.5](https://github.com/OpenMined/PySyft) (privacy-preserving ML with OpenDP). As of 2026, Google research demonstrates user-level DP fine-tuning requires significantly less noise than previously thought, and DP-ZeRO enables large-scale DP training via DeepSpeed with reduced memory overhead. Practical note: DP-SGD adds 3-5x training overhead for full training, though parameter-efficient fine-tuning (LoRA, P-tuning) substantially narrows the gap.
- **Model watermarking:** [Google SynthID](https://deepmind.google/technologies/synthid/) (production on Gemini — text/image/audio/video; text component [open-sourced](https://github.com/google-deepmind/synthid-text)), academic approaches (Kirchenbauer green-list, frequency-domain embedding). Newer "holographic" watermarking distributes marks across entire artifacts, making partial removal destructive. [zkDL++](https://openreview.net/forum?id=JyrjeQJ8VK) (ICLR 2025) introduces zero-knowledge proofs for provable watermark extraction — verifying watermark presence without revealing extractor internals, which closes a key privacy gap in deployment. However, research shows any image watermark can be removed >50% of the time without knowledge of the watermark design, including SynthID and Meta's Stable Signature.
- **Model extraction detection:** [Lakera Guard](https://www.lakera.ai/) (acquired by Check Point 2025 — 98%+ detection, sub-50ms, 100+ languages), rate limiting, query-pattern analysis, model fingerprinting techniques
- **Inference-time anomaly detection:** [NeMo Guardrails](https://docs.nvidia.com/nemo/guardrails/) (NVIDIA — programmable rails with Garak integration), [Rebuff](https://github.com/protectai/rebuff) (multi-layer prompt injection detection), [RAGForensics](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.21668) (first traceback system for RAG poisoning — iteratively retrieves and probes knowledge base subsets to identify poisoned texts; ACM Web Conference 2025), [SDAG](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.04711v2) (Sparse Document Attention — block-sparse attention mask that prevents cross-attention between retrieved documents, requiring only inference-time attention mask changes with no fine-tuning), [ReliabilityRAG](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2509.23519) (builds a contradiction graph over retrieved documents using NLI, selects a consistent majority via Maximum Independent Set to filter poisoned entries), [NPAS + AV Filter](https://openreview.net/forum?id=PS43wqCSME) ("Through the Stealth Lens", OpenReview 2026 — Normalized Passage Attention Score plus Attention-Variance Filter use cross-attention anomalies to flag poisoned retrieved passages; reports ~20% ASR reduction over baseline defenses)
- **Formal robustness verification:** [alpha-beta-CROWN](https://github.com/Verified-Intelligence/alpha-beta-CROWN) (VNN-COMP winner 2021-2025, GPU-accelerated), [auto_LiRPA 0.3](https://github.com/Verified-Intelligence/auto_LiRPA) (provable output bounds), [ERAN](https://github.com/eth-sri/eran) (ETH Zurich — abstract interpretation). *Limitation: scales to small/medium networks; not yet practical for full-scale LLMs.*

### Implementation Maturity

| Control Area | Tooling Maturity | Notes |
|--------------|:---:|-------|
| C11.1 Model Alignment & Safety | Maturing | HarmBench, Garak, PyRIT, JALMBench (ICLR 2026) provide standardized red-teaming. ProAct and HoneyTrap introduce proactive deceptive defenses against multi-turn jailbreaks. Evaluation awareness/sandbagging (C11.1.5) has detection methods (noise injection) but no production tooling. |
| C11.2 Adversarial-Example Hardening | Mature | IBM ART provides comprehensive attack/defense library. Formal verification (C11.2.5) limited to small networks — alpha-beta-CROWN is state of the art but doesn't scale to LLMs. |
| C11.3 Membership-Inference Mitigation | Maturing | Opacus + dp-transformers enable DP fine-tuning. Shadow-model MIA testing is well-documented. 3-5x training overhead remains a practical barrier for full pretraining. |
| C11.4 Model-Inversion Resistance | Maturing | DP training, output calibration, rate limiting are established defenses. The $200 ChatGPT extraction shows alignment doesn't prevent memorization leakage. |
| C11.5 Model-Extraction Defense | Maturing | Lakera Guard, rate limiting, fingerprinting provide detection. SynthID watermarking is production-grade for Google models. Industrial-scale distillation (DeepSeek) shows determined attackers can still succeed. |
| C11.6 Inference-Time Poisoned-Data Detection | Emerging → Early Maturing | PoisonedRAG demonstrates the threat. As of early 2026, three distinct academic defenses have emerged: RAGForensics (traceback to identify poisoned texts), SDAG (sparse attention isolation between documents), and ReliabilityRAG (contradiction-graph filtering). NeMo Guardrails can gate suspicious inputs. No single production-grade solution yet, but the defense surface is expanding. |
| C11.7 Security Policy Adaptation | Mature | Hot-reloadable policy configs are standard practice. Cryptographic signing of policy updates (C11.7.2) uses established tooling. |
| C11.8 Agent Security Self-Assessment | Emerging | NeMo Guardrails and custom policy engines can check planned actions. No dominant open-source tool for agent action review. Morris II worm shows self-assessment can be bypassed. |
| C11.9 Self-Modification & Autonomous Update Security | Emerging | Bounded self-modification (C11.9.4) is largely custom-built. Safety feedback poisoning (C11.9.5) has no standard detection tooling. |
| C11.10 Adversarial Bias Exploitation Defense | Emerging | Subgroup-stratified robustness evaluation (C11.10.2) requires custom benchmarking. No off-the-shelf tool for bias probing detection (C11.10.1). |

## Related Standards & Cross-References

- [NIST AI 100-2 E2025](https://csrc.nist.gov/pubs/ai/100/2/e2025/final) (March 2025, supersedes E2023) — Adversarial ML taxonomy: evasion, poisoning, privacy attacks. New GenAI categories: indirect prompt injection, misaligned outputs, energy-latency attacks.
- [MITRE ATLAS](https://atlas.mitre.org/) (February 2026 v5.4.0: 16 tactics, 84 techniques, 56 sub-techniques, 32 mitigations, 42 case studies; added "Publish Poisoned AI Agent Tool" and "Escape to Host" in 2026 on top of the October 2025 expansion that introduced 14 AI-agent techniques with Zenity Labs) — AML.T0015 (Evade ML Model), AML.T0018 (Backdoor ML Model — .000 Poison, .001 Inject Payload), AML.T0024 (Exfiltration via Inference API — .000 Membership Inference, .001 Model Inversion, .002 Model Extraction), AML.T0043 (Craft Adversarial Data — .000-.004), AML.T0006 (Active Scanning). Case studies: AML.CS0003 (Cylance bypass), deepfake KYC liveness detection bypass (November 2025). STIX 2.1 feed available for machine-readable integration with threat-intel tooling.
- [OWASP LLM02:2025 Sensitive Information Disclosure](https://genai.owasp.org/llmrisk/llm022025-sensitive-information-disclosure/) — PII leakage, prompt leakage, IP exposure via model outputs
- [OWASP LLM04:2025 Data and Model Poisoning](https://genai.owasp.org/llmrisk/llm042025-data-and-model-poisoning/) — Training, fine-tuning, RAG, and embedding poisoning
- [OWASP LLM10:2025 Unbounded Consumption](https://genai.owasp.org/llmrisk/llm102025-unbounded-consumption/) — Resource exhaustion enabling model extraction
- [OWASP Top 10 for Agentic Applications 2026 (ASI)](https://genai.owasp.org/resource/owasp-top-10-for-agentic-applications-for-2026/) (December 2025) — First dedicated framework for autonomous/agentic AI risk. Direct C11 overlaps: ASI01 Agent Goal Hijack, ASI06 Memory & Context Poisoning, ASI07 Insecure Inter-Agent Communication, ASI08 Cascading Failures, ASI10 Rogue Agents. - [EU AI Act Article 15](https://artificialintelligenceact.eu/article/15/) — Accuracy, robustness, and cybersecurity for high-risk AI. Explicitly names 4 attack types: data poisoning, model poisoning, adversarial examples, confidentiality attacks. Becomes enforceable 2 August 2026 for Annex III high-risk categories (hiring, credit scoring, biometrics, critical infrastructure, education, law enforcement, migration/border, essential services); expected evidence includes documented adversarial checks, drift monitoring, and pre-release + periodic testing against the four named attack types.
- [ISO/IEC TR 24029-1](https://www.iso.org/standard/42001) — Robustness of Neural Networks: principles for evaluating behavior under perturbation, model stability, and vulnerability to unexpected inputs
- [ISO/IEC 23894](https://www.iso.org/standard/42001) — AI Risk Management: requires robustness considerations, continuous testing including adversarial scenarios and edge cases
- [CSA AI Controls Matrix](https://cloudsecurityalliance.org/artifacts/ai-controls-matrix) (Jul 2025) — 243 controls including Model Security, Adversarial Defenses, and Model Robustness domains
- [NIST AI RMF](https://www.nist.gov/itl/ai-risk-management-framework) — MEASURE function covers adversarial probing and stress testing; MANAGE covers risk treatment

## Open Research Questions

- [ ] **How do you detect evaluation awareness / sandbagging?** — Frontier models can strategically underperform on dangerous capability evaluations while maintaining general performance. Noise injection into model weights reveals sandbagging through anomalous performance *improvements*, but this isn't a production detection method. Claude 3.5 Sonnet spontaneously underperformed without explicit instruction when it discovered an unlearning process would be triggered.
- [ ] **Can formal verification scale to production LLMs?** — alpha-beta-CROWN wins VNN-COMP annually but only scales to small/medium networks. Certified robustness for full-scale transformers remains an open problem. C11.2.5 requires formal verification for safety-critical components — the "where the model architecture supports them" qualifier is doing heavy lifting.
- [ ] **How do you defend against inference-time RAG poisoning?** — PoisonedRAG showed 5 crafted documents manipulate 90% of responses. As of early 2026, three academic defenses have emerged — RAGForensics (traceback), SDAG (sparse attention isolation), and ReliabilityRAG (contradiction-graph filtering) — but none has reached production maturity. The fundamental challenge remains: models can't distinguish instructions from data in retrieved context, and existing defenses have proven insufficient against sophisticated adaptive attacks.
- [ ] **Is DP-SGD practical for LLM pretraining?** — 3-5x overhead makes DP-SGD feasible for fine-tuning (Opacus + LoRA) but prohibitive for full pretraining. Google's 2026 research on user-level DP shows prior approaches added far more noise than necessary, and DP-ZeRO improves scalability via DeepSpeed. Still, membership inference defenses (C11.3) remain practically limited to fine-tuning scenarios for large models.
- [ ] **Can model watermarking survive determined removal?** — SynthID text watermarking is robust to mild paraphrasing but less effective on factual outputs. Research shows any image watermark (including SynthID and Meta's Stable Signature) can be removed >50% of the time without knowledge of the watermark design. zkDL++ (ICLR 2025) introduces zero-knowledge proofs for provable extraction, which helps verification but doesn't solve the removal problem. Weight-based watermarking for IP protection is an active research area but no standard exists.
- [ ] **Can adversarial robustness keep pace with automated attack generation?** — Automated red-teaming tools discover 37% more unique vulnerabilities than manual testing alone (Mindgard 2026), yet only 26% of organizations conduct proactive security testing. The gap between attack automation (FuzzyAI, JBFuzz, Mastermind) and defense deployment suggests most production systems are undertested. Multi-turn attacks that self-improve through interaction (Mastermind framework) represent a qualitative shift from static jailbreak templates.
- [ ] **How do you bound self-modification in agentic systems?** — Morris II demonstrated autonomous propagation via metamorphic prompts. Copilot configuration self-modification showed silent settings changes. C11.9.4 requires bounded self-modification, but defining and enforcing those bounds at runtime remains unsolved.

## Industry Adoption & Testing Metrics

As of April 2026, industry benchmarks paint a sobering picture of adversarial robustness in practice:

- **Testing coverage gap:** Only 26% of organizations conduct proactive AI security testing. Automated red teaming identifies 37% more unique vulnerabilities than manual testing alone, and post-implementation testing reveals 75-80% more vulnerabilities than pre-deployment assessments catch.
- **Attack success rates in benchmarked evaluations:** Role-play attacks succeed at 89.6%, multi-turn jailbreaks reach 97% within five turns, and healthcare-domain LLM attacks hit 94.4% adversarial objective completion. Even simple cognitive manipulations ("bad math" misdirection) succeed 76% of the time.
- **Detection capability:** Best-in-class model detection (Claude 3.7 Sonnet) achieves only 46.9% detection rate against adversarial inputs. Known jailbreak exploits succeed at a 17% average rate even against models with active defenses, and documented attack techniques show 28% overall success.
- **Economic impact:** Organizations with mature AI security practices experience 60% fewer incidents. Proactive testing yields an estimated $2.4 million in prevented breach costs, with roughly $1.9 million saved per breach avoided.
- **Framework expansion:** MITRE ATLAS grew from its initial scope to 15 tactics and 66 techniques by October 2025, adding 14 AI agent-specific techniques. AI-assisted cyber attacks surged 72% in 2025, driving the need for more comprehensive adversarial robustness frameworks. The 2026 ATLAS update shifts focus from model-centric attacks to execution-layer exposure, covering autonomous workflow chaining, delegated authority persistence, and API-level orchestration risk.
- **CrowdStrike 2026 threat data:** 89% increase in AI-enabled attacks year-over-year. Average eCrime breakout time dropped to 29 minutes (65% faster than 2024), with the fastest observed at 27 seconds. Adversaries exploited legitimate GenAI tools at 90+ organizations via malicious prompt injection. FANCY BEAR deployed LLM-enabled malware (LAMEHUG) for automated recon; FAMOUS CHOLLIMA used AI-generated personas for insider operations at scale.
- **Emerging defensive results:** ProAct and HoneyTrap represent a shift toward proactive, deceptive defense. ProAct's spurious response strategy reduces ASR by up to 92% (to 0% when combined with other defenses). HoneyTrap's multi-agent honeypot forces 19.8x attacker resource consumption. TBOP (April 2026) claims ASR of 2.56% with 60x faster inference than the next-best defense. These approaches complement rather than replace static alignment — the defense-in-depth picture is maturing.
- **Defense claims need adaptive-attack validation:** A March 2026 replication study broke 12 of 12 published AI defenses that had each claimed near-zero ASR, restoring observed success rates to attack-level baselines. This is the direct justification for C11.2.4 (adaptive attacks) and C11.6.4 (adaptive stress tests) — authors' own benchmark numbers are not sufficient evidence, and teams should re-validate any defense against adaptive attacks tailored to the specific deployed configuration before relying on it.

These numbers reinforce that adversarial robustness is not a theoretical concern — it's a measurable operational risk with quantifiable return on investment for organizations that test proactively.
