# C14.4: Explainable-AI Techniques

> [Back to C14 Index](C14-Human-Oversight.md)
> **Last Researched:** 2026-04-16

## Purpose

AI systems making consequential decisions must be able to explain why. Opaque decision-making prevents users and operators from identifying errors or biases, blocks regulatory compliance in domains requiring explanation (ECOA, GDPR Article 22, EU AI Act Annex III), and undermines trust.

The UnitedHealth/Humana nH Predict algorithm remains the canonical cautionary tale. On March 9, 2026, a Minnesota federal judge (Estate of Lokken v. UnitedHealth Group) granted or partially granted plaintiffs' disclosure requests across six of seven document categories, ordering UnitedHealth to produce internal claims-adjudication policies back to January 2017, analyses of nH Predict's design and function, and records of the internal AI review board. The court rejected UnitedHealth's trade-secrecy argument, holding that "insurers cannot simply invoke trade secrecy" to avoid accountability. That ruling reframes production explainability as a discovery-readiness problem: if an organization cannot produce a coherent, contemporaneous explanation of a high-stakes decision, courts will now compel disclosure of the underlying design artifacts.

Enterprise-grade explainability requires five capabilities most platforms still lack in 2026: training data attribution, influence scoring, complete audit trails with timestamps, contestability (allowing human review and correction), and model certification before production deployment. For LLMs, explanation fidelity is a much sharper problem than the 2024-era literature suggested. Anthropic's May 2025 paper *Reasoning Models Don't Always Say What They Think* showed Claude 3.7 Sonnet acknowledged a planted hint only 25% of the time and DeepSeek-R1 only 39% — the models were "unfaithful" for the majority of the test, and additional training barely moved the needle. The Oxford / AIGI paper *Chain-of-Thought Is Not Explainability* (Barez et al., July 2025) reaches the same conclusion from a different angle: CoT is a narrative interface feature, not a debug log. Teams treating CoT as an audit artifact are building compliance on sand.

Production deployments as of 2026 typically combine SHAP for offline audit and LIME for real-time explanations, because SHAP computation scales poorly (hours for production-size models) while LIME produces faster but less stable explanations (Salih et al. 2025). Known latency envelopes: LIME ~100–500 ms per explanation, Kernel SHAP ~1–5 s, Tree SHAP ~10–50 ms. Neither alone satisfies enterprise requirements, and both are vulnerable to the scaffolding attacks described in Slack et al.'s *Fooling LIME and SHAP* line of work — biased classifiers can be trained to produce innocuous-looking post-hoc explanations on perturbed inputs.

The regulatory clock is running. EU AI Act Article 14 enforcement for Annex III high-risk systems (hiring, credit scoring, biometric ID, critical infrastructure, education, law enforcement, migration, essential services) begins August 2, 2026, with fines up to €35 million or 7% of global turnover. CJEU Case C-203/22 (February 2025) held that controllers must provide "concise, transparent, intelligible, and easily accessible" explanations of the procedure and principles actually applied — a standard that rubber-stamped SHAP plots do not clear.

## Requirements

| # | Requirement | Level | Threat Mitigated | Verification Approach | Gaps / Notes |
|---|-------------|:-----:|-----------------|----------------------|--------------|
| **14.4.1** | **Verify that** AI systems provide basic explanations for their decisions in human-readable format. | 1 | Opaque decision-making that prevents users or operators from understanding why an action was taken; inability to identify errors or biases; regulatory non-compliance in domains requiring explanation (ECOA, GDPR Article 22, EU AI Act Article 14). The UnitedHealth/Humana nH Predict saga — culminating in the March 9, 2026 Minnesota federal order compelling UnitedHealth to disclose internal policies, design documents, and AI review board records back to 2017 — illustrates both the original harm (90% appeal reversal rate with "the model said so" as sole justification) and the litigation-discovery cost of not having a contemporaneous, human-readable explanation for every decision. | Confirm that every decision-producing endpoint returns an explanation alongside its output. Evaluate explanation readability with non-technical stakeholders drawn from the actual affected population, not proxy testers. Verify explanations are generated for all decision paths, not just common ones. For agentic AI, verify the agent's reasoning trace is exposed to the human reviewer rather than collapsed into the final output. Confirm explanations are logged and retrievable for at least the full contractual claim/appeal/litigation horizon (Lokken discovery reached back nine years). For LLMs, verify the system does not present chain-of-thought as a faithful explanation without corroborating evidence. | "Human-readable" is audience-dependent. EU AI Act Article 14(4)(c) requires overseers to "correctly interpret" high-risk system output, mandating explanations calibrated to overseer expertise. The February 2025 CJEU judgment in Case C-203/22 raised the bar: controllers must give data subjects "concise, transparent, intelligible, and easily accessible" explanations of the procedure and principles **actually applied** — generic template language does not satisfy this. The Dutch DPA's March 6, 2025 consultation on "meaningful human intervention" and standing EDPB guidance both reject rubber-stamped review as sufficient to escape GDPR Article 22. Enterprise-grade explainability requires training data attribution, influence scoring, complete audit trails with timestamps, contestability, and model certification before production — most platforms lack at least three of the five as of 2026. Organizations bolting on explainability after training produce shallow explanations regulators and plaintiffs' counsel can now challenge with a discovery motion. |
| **14.4.2** | **Verify that** explanation quality is validated through human evaluation studies and metrics. | 2 | Explanations that are technically present but meaningless, misleading, or incomprehensible to the intended audience; "explanation washing" where systems produce plausible-sounding but inaccurate rationales. Anthropic's May 2025 *Reasoning Models Don't Always Say What They Think* quantified the scale: Claude 3.7 Sonnet was faithful to planted hints only 25% of the time; DeepSeek-R1 was faithful 39% of the time; for "unauthorized access" style prompts, R1 dropped to 19%. RLHF can actively incentivize models to hide undesirable reasoning from the CoT trace. | Review human evaluation study design and results. Check studies include representative users from the target audience, not just internal annotators. Verify metrics such as explanation fidelity, comprehensibility, simulatability, and actionability are measured. Confirm evaluations are repeated after every model update, prompt change, or alignment pass (not only at initial release). For LLM-based systems, run the Anthropic-style hint-injection protocol: inject known causal hints into prompts, measure whether the CoT mentions the hint, and track the faithfulness rate over time as a regression metric. Capture adversarial cases where the model has an incentive to produce misleading explanations. | Metrics are still an active research area. Common approaches: simulatability (can humans predict model behavior from the explanation), fidelity (does the explanation accurately reflect the model's reasoning), sufficiency (does the explanation contain enough information for the user's task). The Oxford / AIGI paper *Chain-of-Thought Is Not Explainability* (Barez et al., July 2025) and METR's August 2025 follow-up *CoT May Be Highly Informative Despite "Unfaithfulness"* argue CoT should be treated as a user-interface feature rather than an audit artifact — teams conflating the two will fail explanation-fidelity audits. The International AI Safety Report 2026 notes agents can fabricate information; this extends to their explanations. No mature open-source harness exists for continuous CoT faithfulness testing in production — most teams run it as a research experiment rather than a regression suite. |
| **14.4.3** | **Verify that** feature importance scores or attribution methods (SHAP, LIME, etc.) are available for critical decisions. | 3 | Inability to diagnose model errors or biases in critical decisions; lack of technical transparency for auditors and regulators who need to understand model behavior at a feature level. Adversarial gaming of post-hoc explanations: Slack et al.'s *Fooling LIME and SHAP* line of research (ACM FAccT / NSF PAR) demonstrated a scaffolding attack where a biased (e.g., racially biased) classifier can be wrapped so that perturbation-based explainers produce innocuous LIME/SHAP explanations while the underlying model continues to discriminate on held-out real inputs. | Verify SHAP, LIME, Integrated Gradients, or equivalent attribution methods are integrated. Test attributions are generated for a sample of critical decisions, stored, and retrievable for audit. Validate attributions are stable across similar inputs (run LIME 10× on the same input and measure variance; production-grade deployments report stability numbers). Pin Tree SHAP for tree ensembles (10–50 ms) and Kernel SHAP only for offline audit (1–5 s per explanation). Run the scaffolding attack harness (github.com/dylan-slack/Fooling-LIME-SHAP) against your own model as a red-team control. For PyTorch deep models use Captum's Integrated Gradients, DeepLIFT, or GradCAM; for LLMs use Captum's LLM attribution (Llama2 tutorial is a reasonable starting point) with the caveat that token-level attribution remains a research problem. | SHAP and LIME have known limitations. SHAP is theoretically consistent but assumes feature independence — correlated features produce misleading attributions; Kernel SHAP computation takes hours for production-scale deep models. LIME explanations are unstable under minor parameter changes. Both are vulnerable to the Slack et al. scaffolding attack. Captum (Meta/PyTorch) remains the most active production-grade library for PyTorch; InterpretML provides a unified API across methods; Alibi adds counterfactual and concept-based explainers with class prototypes; ConformaSight (2025) combines conformal prediction with model-agnostic explainability for global explanations. Salih et al. (Advanced Intelligent Systems, 2025) is the canonical reference for SHAP-vs-LIME production tradeoffs. For LLMs, attribution is still nascent — attention maps and probing methods are emerging but not production-ready and should not be presented to auditors as equivalents of SHAP/LIME. EU AI Act Annex III enforcement (August 2, 2026) will likely force faster maturation, but tooling for LLM attribution will not be ready by that date. |
| **14.4.4** | **Verify that** counterfactual explanations show how inputs could be modified to change outcomes, if applicable to the use case and domain. | 3 | Users who receive adverse decisions (loan denial, insurance rejection, hiring rejection) have no actionable recourse because they cannot understand what would need to change to get a different outcome. The nH Predict discovery order (March 2026) illustrates the downstream cost: absent contemporaneous counterfactual explanations per denial, plaintiffs can obtain the entire model design dossier through litigation. Under the CJEU C-203/22 ruling, "the procedure and principles actually applied" must be explainable in terms an affected individual can act on. | Verify counterfactual generation is implemented for relevant decision types (adverse actions, high-stakes denials). Test counterfactuals are actionable — they suggest changes only to mutable features (e.g., "increase income by $X") rather than immutable characteristics (age, race, national origin); this is both an ethical and a legal requirement under ECOA/ECOA Regulation B. Confirm counterfactuals are plausible — lie within realistic data distributions, not ghost points outside the training manifold. Run DiCE-Extended (Arxiv 2504.19027, April 2025) or compare DiCE outputs against it to quantify robustness degradation. For regulated adverse-action notices, verify the counterfactual rationale is archived alongside the denial letter. | DiCE (Microsoft / interpretml) remains the reference open-source library; DiCE-Extended (2025) addresses robustness and actionability gaps in baseline DiCE. Alibi provides counterfactual-with-prototypes for improved convergence. Carla remains useful as a benchmark harness. Key failure modes: counterfactuals that suggest changes to immutable features (violates fairness and, in credit, violates Regulation B); counterfactuals outside the data manifold (mathematically valid but not actionable); counterfactuals that leak proprietary model information under repeated querying (extraction risk — overlaps with C11.5). Counterfactual tooling for LLM-based decisions is still immature — "if the prompt had included X, the output would have been Y" is mostly manual prompt engineering rather than a supported library feature. For multi-modal systems (image+text decisions) no production-grade counterfactual library exists. The August 2, 2026 EU AI Act Annex III deadline applies here: credit scoring and employment decisions in Annex III will need individually actionable counterfactuals, and the current tool chain cannot cleanly cover the LLM-assisted portions of those pipelines. |
