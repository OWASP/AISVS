# C3.2: Model Validation & Testing

> [Back to C03 Index](C03-Model-Lifecycle-Management.md)
> **Last Researched:** 2026-03-29

## Purpose

Models must pass defined security and safety validations before deployment. This section covers the pre-deployment testing gates, audit logging of model changes, override processes, and the re-evaluation requirements for models that undergo post-training compression (quantization, pruning, distillation).

Pre-deployment testing is the primary defense against deploying models vulnerable to prompt injection, jailbreaks, or models that produce unsafe outputs. Despite this, Cleanlab's 2025 production survey found only 5% of AI agents in production have mature monitoring -- underscoring the critical need for automated testing gates before models ever reach production. Post-training compression techniques, increasingly common for edge deployment and cost reduction, can silently degrade safety alignment and adversarial robustness, requiring distinct re-evaluation of compressed artifacts.
